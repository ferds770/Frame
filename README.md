## 1. What FRAME Is

FRAME is the witnessed declaration layer for accountable human origin in the age of machine intelligence.

It provides procedural scaffolding to ensure events, actions, and constructs remain intelligible, traceable, and constitutionally accountable.

At its core, FRAME preserves accountable human declaration, witness, intelligibility, provenance, and constitutional legitimacy.

---

## 2. What FRAME Is Not

FRAME is intentionally scoped to address specific constitutional concerns. It does not claim to solve every problem of legitimacy, truth, identity, or governance.

FRAME is not:

* **A truth engine**: FRAME adjudicates procedure, not truth.
* **A blockchain replacement**: Blockchain may preserve transaction history; FRAME preserves accountable human origin and witness.
* **An incorruptibility machine**: FRAME guarantees intelligibility, not incorruptibility.
* **An automated legitimacy machine**: Legitimacy still requires human declaration, witness, and constitutional process.
* **A coercive identity system**: FRAME safeguards human agency while preserving accountable origin.
* **An AI governance replacement**: AI may assist, simulate, or propose, but it cannot originate constitutional authority.
* **A substitute for human judgment**: Procedural accountability preserves intelligibility, while ultimate authority remains human.

FRAME operates as a bounded constitutional onboarding scaffold for human-origin governance.

---

## 3. Core Constitutional Invariants

FRAME relies on these immutable principles:

### Human-Origin Principle

Legitimate consequential authority originates in conscious human declaration, attestation, and accountable witness.

### Human-Origin Precedes Legitimate Consequence

Actions enabled by FRAME require accountable human origin for legitimacy. Machine inference alone cannot establish constitutional validity.

### The Circuit Breaker Belongs to Humanity

Human beings retain authority to interrupt, reject, challenge, pause, or redirect consequential systems.

### Detection Is a Machine Function; Consequentiality Is a Constitutional Determination

Machines may detect, classify, or infer operations, but only human-origin authority determines legitimate constitutional consequence.

### FRAME Adjudicates Procedure, Not Truth

FRAME preserves intelligible procedure for accountability while declining to adjudicate broader truth claims.

### Intelligibility, Not Incorruptibility

FRAME does not guarantee immunity from corruption.

FRAME guarantees that legitimacy remains intelligible, witnessable, and contestable under constitutional scrutiny.

---

## 4. Human-Origin in Practice

FRAME preserves the requirement that legitimate consequential authority remain accountable to conscious human declaration.

In practice this means:

* **Consequential legitimacy requires accountable human origin**
* **Machine inference alone is insufficient** for constitutional legitimacy
* **Witness, attestation, and provenance** anchor actions alongside technical capability
* **Capability ≠ Legitimacy**: technical execution does not imply constitutional legitimacy

FRAME separates technical capability from legitimate authority.

## 5. Six-Tuple Event Record

The Six-Tuple Event Record defines FRAME declarations in minimal accountable terms:

1. **Event** — what occurred
2. **Author** — who initiated the declaration
3. **Time** — when the event occurred
4. **Intent** — purpose or rationale
5. **Value / Governing Principle** — constitutional frame of reference
6. **Signature** — verification of authorship and witness

## 7. F-Witness / Witness Layer

F-Witness carries witness across systems while preserving constitutional boundaries.

### Key Principles

* **Read, not run**: F-Witness packets are read, not executed.
* **Preserves provenance**: Encodes declaration, lineage, and authority references.
* **Does not originate legitimacy**: Witness ensures traceability of declarations but does not gate, verify, or execute downstream actions.
* **Separate concerns**: FRAME separates declaration from execution.

Execution and legitimacy require distinct constitutional processes.

## 10. Logos → Manifestation

FRAME traces the sequence of meaning becoming actionable:

**Declaration → Logos → Manifestation**

* **Declaration** — conscious statement or intent
* **Logos** — meaning rendered intelligible and witnessable
* **Manifestation** — meaning enacted through legitimate consequence

FRAME ensures intelligibility persists from declaration to manifestation.

A declaration that cannot be understood cannot be witnessed.

A witness that cannot be understood cannot anchor accountability.

The constitutional status of Logos is resolved: its philosophical argument functions as binding interpretive doctrine, while theological expression remains manifesto-tier language.

## 11. Trifecta Process

The Trifecta adversarially reviews FRAME proposals to preserve constitutional integrity.

### Key Principles

* **Functional roles only**: AI may review but cannot originate authority.
* **Human-origin ratifies**: Sole authority rests with human ratifiers.
* **Open tensions remain visible**: unresolved tensions should not be prematurely smoothed into false certainty.
* **Disputed claims require source verification**: doctrine, artifacts, or source material should be consulted where possible.

## 12. Bounded Legitimacy Preservation

FRAME does not guarantee incorruptibility.

FRAME preserves the intelligibility of legitimacy under constitutional scrutiny.

This principle emerged from bounded legitimacy stress testing and reflects a constitutional constraint:

> FRAME adjudicates procedure, not truth.

## 13. Known Boundaries & Open Tensions

### Wire Problem

The Wire Problem defines the boundary of FRAME’s scope.

Execution layers exist outside FRAME.

The remaining challenge is external integration, systemic adoption, and constitutional consequence gating across downstream environments.

### Capture at Scale

FRAME preserves bounded legitimacy under institutional degradation, but legitimacy preservation at large-scale social or institutional capture remains unresolved.

### Predicate Constraint

Predicate and observational logic may support intelligibility and reasoning but do not independently authorize constitutional consequence.
